FAQ

Seriously, why wouldn't someone just donate their items to established charities?
We have no issue at all with established charities that accept goods as donations. We donate to them too. But, there are some items that people have that mean a lot to them, but that they still need to get rid of and we are offering another outlet for distribution of those items in a more personal manner. My grandmother made a wicked cool ski hat for me when I was a teenager. She spent a lot of time working on it and it is really special to me, but as a new dad with limited space in my home I need to get rid of items regardless of sentimental value and I'd prefer to give that hat to a ski junkie in Colorado rather than someone shopping for goofy hats at goodwill.
Can you expand upon your concerns with ebay, craigslist and freecycle?
I love auctions and estate sales. Sometimes at auction items will sell at a real discount to their value. So, I have bought those items and flipped them on ebay. With ebay, you will pay fees and also risk dealing with very challenging buyers that threaten you with bad feedback or can claim they never received an item and get a full refund from you through paypal. The first time you spend a lot of time listing an item, then carefully packaging and shipping the item, only to have an unscrupulous buyer take advantage of you, will you appreciate the hassle that getting rid of items through ebay can cause. As for Craigslist and Freecycle, both are great and I've had very pleasant encounters with people using those services. However, I've had to weed through spam emails and emails where people misrepresent their situation in order to get free items of value from people who are simply trying to help out a stranger. Also, there are very few instances where a charity would prefer you by an item new for them vs. providing them with a cash donation. However, Give Stuff. Feel Good. allows you to purchase an item for the simple joy of giving it to someone you personally feel will benefit from it.
How will Give Stuff. Feel Good. Be different than other giving sites?
We will learn from the lessons of those sites that came before us and build upon the foundation they laid by improving:
  • Recipient user registration
  • Metrics to reduce gift trolling
  • Feedback process for giving/recieving
  • Social proof
  • Ease of listing
  • Incentivizing listing (PIF requests, etc)
  • and more!
Who is really going to just give stuff to strangers?
We like to believe that for the most part, people don't suck. We're willing to put in some effort to see just how true our theory is. :)
